How is it calculated?

A carbon footprint is calculated by estimating the total amount of greenhouse gases (GHGs) emitted directly or indirectly by human activities, typically measured in units of carbon dioxide equivalents (CO2e). Here's a general outline of how it's done:

Identify Activities:

Determine the activities that contribute to GHG emissions. Common sources include electricity consumption, transportation (car, air travel), heating (natural gas, oil), waste production, and industrial processes.

Collect Data:

Gather data on the amount of resources consumed or the extent of activities. For example, the number of kilowatt-hours (kWh) of electricity used, miles driven, gallons of gasoline consumed, hours of flight time, or tons of waste produced.

Emission Factors:

Use emission factors to convert activity data into CO2e. Emission factors represent the average emissions produced per unit of activity. For instance, the amount of CO2 emitted per kWh of electricity, per mile driven, per gallon of gasoline, per hour of flight, or per ton of waste.
