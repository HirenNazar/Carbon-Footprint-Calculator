What is a Carbon Footprint?


A carbon footprint is the total amount of greenhouse gases (GHGs) emitted directly or indirectly by an individual, organization, event, or product throughout its lifecycle. These emissions are typically measured in units of carbon dioxide equivalents (CO₂e) to account for the varying global warming potentials of different GHGs, such as methane (CH₄) and nitrous oxide (N₂O)


Measuring your carbon footprint is crucial for mitigating climate change, as it helps identify and reduce greenhouse gas emissions. By understanding your impact, you can adopt more sustainable practices, save on energy costs, comply with environmental regulations, and enhance your reputation. This awareness enables informed decisions that promote long-term environmental health and contribute to a sustainable future.
